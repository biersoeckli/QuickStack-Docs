# Expose Your App: Domain + HTTPS

Once your app is deployed, expose it to the internet with a domain and automatic HTTPS.

## Option A: QuickStack.me (No DNS setup required)

The fastest way to get a working HTTPS URL without owning a domain.

1. Open your app → **Domains** tab.
2. Click **Add Domain** → switch to the **quickstack.me Domain** tab.
3. Enter a unique prefix (e.g. `my-app`).
4. Set **App Port** to the port your container listens on (default: `80`).
5. Enable **Use HTTPS** and **Redirect HTTP to HTTPS**.
6. Click **Save**.
7. Click **Deploy** to apply the changes.

Your app is now live at `https://<prefix>.<hex-ip>.quickstack.me`.

→ [Full quickstack.me docs](/docs/how-to/networking/quickstack-me)

## Option B: Custom Domain

Requires a domain you own with DNS access.

### 1. Point DNS to your server

Create an `A record` at your DNS provider pointing your domain (or subdomain) to your server's public IP address.

### 2. Add the domain in QuickStack

1. Open your app → **Domains** tab.
2. Click **Add Domain**.
3. Enter your **Hostname** (e.g. `app.example.com`).
4. Set **App Port** to the port your container listens on.
5. Enable **Use HTTPS** (Let's Encrypt certificate provisioned automatically).
6. Enable **Redirect HTTP to HTTPS**.
7. Click **Save**.
8. Click **Deploy** to apply the changes.

:::info Deploy Required
Configuration changes in QuickStack do not take effect until you click **Deploy**.
:::

→ [Full custom domain docs](/docs/how-to/networking/domains)

## Troubleshooting

- **SSL certificate validation fails:** DNS propagation can take a few minutes. Also ensure Let's Encrypt email is configured in **Settings → QuickStack Server → Networking / Traefik**.
- **App not accessible:** Verify your DNS A record points to the correct server IP.
- **Wrong port:** Check your container's listening port and update the App Port accordingly.
